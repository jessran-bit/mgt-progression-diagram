Color coding follows the official course checklist categories. The diagram shows the term-by-term flow and highlights major prerequisite chains (Accounting, Analytics/Systems, Research/Thesis).

Year of Study Progression Summary
Year Dominant Learning Focus Expected Learning Outcomes Primary Integration Mechanism
Year 1 Foundational business competencies Students demonstrate understanding and initial application of essential business concepts across functional areas (management, accounting, economics, communication). Students begin to use basic ICT tools and articulate ideas in structured academic contexts. Foundational business and general education courses
Year 2 Application and analytical development Students apply business concepts and decision tools to structured organizational problems, analyze issues using basic analytical frameworks, and observe ethical and legal considerations. Collaborative work introduces team-based problem solving. Core business courses and case-based learning
Year 3 Functional and cross-functional integration Students integrate knowledge across functional areas, execute fundamental management functions, and communicate analyses/recommendations to stakeholders. Activities require increasing independence and practical application. Practicum-related courses and integrative business subjects
Year 4 Strategic integration and professional practice Students demonstrate integrated management and leadership competencies through internship and undergraduate thesis. Students strategically plan and execute business activities, apply ethical judgment, and present evidence-based recommendations addressing organizational and societal concerns. Internship and undergraduate thesis
